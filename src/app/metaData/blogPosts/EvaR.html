Meet Eva Robinson, this weeks WCW! Eva is one of our newer climbers, and we've loved watching her learn the ropes and increase her knowledge, skills, and strength over this past year! Eva is a graduate student in the Environmental Education program at UMD so it's not surprising that she is passionate about both improving public education and spending time outside. She loves hiking, backpacking, running, and yoga, and she's also into water sports, such as water skiing, paddle boarding, and her favorite, scuba-diving. After a full day she enjoys cooking and trying new foods, especially when paired with a good wine or beer! Eva has been wonderful to get to know, and we love building relationships and community through this group! What do you love about climbing/what does climbing mean to you? Climbing feels like playing to me.  As adults, and myself in particular, we take often take life so seriously that we forget to play.  When I’m climbing, I feel like I’m 10 years old playing on the rocks while on a hike with my parents. I also don’t think about much else when I’m climbing and I’m usually with another person who’s company I greatly enjoying.  It’s such a good reminder that life is better when we spend time with great people and just play! What made you join/ get involved with VertiGals?: My friend Megan told me about the group and since I had moved to Duluth pretty recently, it seemed like a great way to meet more like-minded people.  I was hooked after the first climb night I attended.  I don’t think I’ve ever felt more welcomed into a new group of people than I have with VertiGals.  Even though I don’t have a lot of climbing experience, I’ve never felt intimidated, only inspired to climb more! [caption id="attachment_592" align="alignnone" width="1583"] Eva's first ever outdoor climb! Shovel Point, Tettagouche State Park, MN. PC Erin Hammes[/caption] Share a story of an impactful climb or experience you've had related to climbing (good or bad), or an experience you're proud of. After meeting Erin at a climb night, and telling her that I had only climbed outside twice, she invited me to climb with her the following Sunday at Shovel Point. Snow flurries were predicted, I had just met Erin, and I had little experience outside.  However, I decided it seemed like a great opportunity and joined in on the adventure.  I was so nervous during my first climb, but Erin was really encouraging and I felt so accomplished when I finally reached the top. I’d had enough adrenaline for the day and spent the rest of the day watching, talking, and enjoying the incredible views of the North Shore.  Even though I only climbed once, it was a great way for me to push myself outside of my comfort zone. How did you get started climbing? I started climbing in high school thorough an elective called Adventure Education.  We learned how to climb on the school’s wall, took a field trip to VE in Minneapolis, and climbed outside at Devil’s Lake.  Looking back, it was a pretty phenomenal opportunity for exposure to new types of activity, especially for a public high school.  Although I loved the class, I didn’t stick with it and only started climbing again about a year ago after moving back to Duluth. My husband and I are both pretty inexperienced climbers so we thought climbing would be a good things for us to grow in and learn together. [caption id="attachment_220" align="alignnone" width="960"] Eva has been attending VertiGals events with us for a while now![/caption] How are you hoping to grow as a climber or what skills are you hoping to develop? Climbing makes me really nervous. Some days when I’m in the gym I feel like I can do anything and other days I feel like I might cry halfway up the wall.  Mentally, I know I’m going to be ok but some days it takes a lot of internal reminders to keep me going.  And sometimes, I just accept that it might not be my day to push myself.  That balance of mental toughness and self-acceptance is something I’m always trying to improve, both on and off of the wall.
