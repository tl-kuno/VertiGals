It's February and so, if you aren't into ice climbing, it is prime-time GYM time. This month, meet Ariel Johnson! You'll see her crushing it at the VE in Duluth. Ariel is a Solon Springs native who is the Director of the Fond du Lac Tribal and Community College's Nandagikendan (Seek to Learn) program. Read more to learn about her!

Fast Facts:

Favorite hobbies: hiking, snowshoeing, fishing, boating, kayaking, gardening, walking with my three dogs, and spending time outdoors with family and friends
Favorite crag elsewhere: I climbed a long time ago in Michigan (the U.P.) and can't for the life of me remember where!
When and where did you originally start climbing: 2015 at Vertical Endeavors
If you had a million dollars, the first big purchase you would make is: A cabin or farmhouse with a lot of land
When travel is unrestricted again, where do you hope to travel? I hope to travel to New Mexico to meet my boyfriend's family.
What's something about you that no one could ever guess? I survived cancer when I was 19.
Job Title: Nandagikendan (Seek to Learn) Academy Director

'What's The Nandagikendan Seek to Learn Academy?', you may ask. (I had to google it, too, dear reader. Don't fret!) The Academy provides high school students the opportunity to learn and experience college life on the Fond du Lac Tribal and Community College campus. So cool!

You work at the Fond du Lac Tribal & Community College - tell us about your job!
I work in student services doing a variety of things from high school programming for Native American students to provide opportunities for them to experience college and career options, I oversee the Ambassador program which focuses on providing educational (and fun!) activities to support student needs and success, and I take on other projects as needed to support our students holistically. I see it as being able to hang out with students and help them in being prepared for whatever comes their way while also being able to create fun and helpful events for them!

This year has been a wild one. What's something you have chosen to focus on as we continue in this Pandemic?

I've chosen to focus on family and turning our house into a home with lots of remodeling! I've been spending a lot of time with my dogs by hiking (and snowshoeing) the trail in our yard. It's hard to bring three dogs to local trails/parks and keep them under control…especially being 20 weeks pregnant!

Right! Okay, so besides bringing LIFE into this world (CONGRATS!!), do you have another "Pandemic Project"?

I just recently started learning how to play guitar. This past year I've been so focused on remodeling our house, self-care (exercising with the dogs is my go-to!) and finishing my master's degree that I finally decided to do something fun that isn't so much a "requirement". It'll help me "slow down" now that I'm getting further into my pregnancy.

Why do you spend your time climbing as opposed to other activities?
I enjoy climbing because it challenges you mentally as well as physically. Some days it feels like a dance up the wall, other days it feels more like moving a mountain.

You you experienced sexism, racism, homophobia, etc. in the outdoors community? What did you take away from that?
While I haven't experienced so much in outdoors communities, I have experienced when I was an intern at a local fishery (my bachelor's is focused in more of the fieldwork biology). I was questioned frequently on if I could handle lifting things or dissecting fish (to gather data for ongoing studies) due to being a female. The other intern and I were the first female interns for that branch, and we felt the need to prove we could do just as well as, if not better than, our male peers.

[W]e felt the need to prove we could do just as well as, if not better than, our male peers.

From that experience in your internship, or your experience from FDL, or honestly just life in general, what would you like to see groups like VertiGals do to be inclusive??
As of right now it would be difficult, but once things start opening up I think Vertigals could partner with other groups or with Vertical Endeavors (or other climbing areas) to host "open climb nights" or other public events to encourage people of all backgrounds to try climbing. It can be intimidating to see more experienced climbers rocking the wall (pun intended), but my experiences have usually been that those people are always more than excited to help newer climbers and encourage them to keep going. I've always found the rock climbing communities to be supportive and diverse, so I think we could just continue that positive vibe of building our community.

We couldn't agree more, Ariel. Here's to more positive vibes in 2021!!!
