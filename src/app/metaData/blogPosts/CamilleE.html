Meet Camille Erickson, longtime North Shore climber and outdoor sports enthusiast! Camille has climbed for FIFTEEN years! Camille is a health coach at Profile by Sanford, has an adorable pup, does ALL of the activities, and is relatable as all heck! Read more about our May #WCW below!

________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Camille, you do so many activities! Tell us how you got into climbing!

I started climbing in 2004 when I worked as a camp counselor for a YMCA camp in Connecticut. I had always liked climbing trees as a kid, so climbing rocks seemed like a fun thing to do. I spent my summers in Connecticut teaching kids to climb and getting in my first few multiple pitches. During the school year I went to UMD so started volunteering at the climbing wall there. Coincidentally, my older brother was getting into climb as well and also lived in Duluth. He was highly influential in showing me all the amazing places to climb around Duluth and up The Shore. One of my first outside climbs up The Shore was at Section 13, which really led me to fall in love with not only that spot but climbing on The Shore in general. This area is like a giant playground and I definitely have more exploring to do.

Ah, a local gal....so you must have a favorite local crag??

I am a big fan of the Mystical Mountain Zone. It was the place that a pulled my first roof, which was a move I was really struggling with. It definitely helped me get over the mental block I had put on myself. It also has some fun hand jams to play on as well.

___

Looks like you've climbed in some very cool spots, tell us about a dreamy experience you've had while climbing....

A few years ago my brother Sam, friend Dan and I were climbing Birdland, a fun 600 foot climb in Red Rocks, near Las Vegas. The wall gets blasted by the sun most of the day, so it can be too hot to climb, especially for this Minnesotan. We set out late in the afternoon and had the climb to ourselves. About halfway up you are standing on a little ledge with nothing but flat rock and crack around you. The exposure was making me a little squirmy – but in a fun way. It was just at this time when the sun set and we clicked on our headlamps. This was my first time climbing in the darkness and I completely fell in love with it! All my squirmy feelings melted away and you only concern yourself with the rock your headlamp can show you. With the lights of Vegas showing in the distance we finished the climbing and packed up to rap down. Dan and Sam simo-rappelled down and then I followed the glow of their headlamps on my own. While waiting at the anchor for my turn, I was struck by the intense calm. I was experiencing a gentle breeze coming across my body, a blanket full of stars above me and 600 feet of darkness below me. It was an incredible experience – one that won’t be forgotten any time soon.

Favorite Inspirational Climber??

Lynn Hill is a complete beast! She was the first person to ever free climb the Nose in Yosemite! It is very inspiring! She was able to do it when everyone else thought it was impossible. She is strong, brave and someone any outdoor enthusiast should aspire to be like.

____

So, you ever had a bad climbing experience??

On my first ever multi-pitch climb out in Connecticut, I was climbing with my good friend Mikey. We were both pretty fresh to multi-pitch but eager to get on the rock. On the second pitch of the climb we had to traverse around a large bulge in the rock and then were meant continue up a chimney to the to the third anchor. Mikey led and had hauled up the rope behind him. I broke down the anchor and precariously started my dance around the bulge. We were around 200 feet off the ground, so there was a bit of exposure for someone who hadn’t done a lot of multi-pitch. Needless to say, I was a little nervous about the traverse. My little dance across the rock was successful but looking into the chimney gave me my real fright! The rope had not been tight on me the entire time, but the weight of the rope as well as rope drag had tricked me into thinking I was on belay! There was about 30 feet of rope hanging down in the chimney and looping back up to me. I squeaked out, perhaps the most, urgent “take” of my life! The rest of the pitch went off without a hitch but I learned my lesson with rope drag and good communication with your belayer that day, for sure!

____

Why do you love climbing?

I think the part of climbing that I enjoy the most is the puzzle, especially when climbing outside. I love how two people can approach a climbing in completely different ways and be successful. A move that may be easy for someone who is tall, makes me have to think creatively. There is always a way to solve the puzzle though.

___

Has anything really held you back in your climbing??

I consider myself a bit of a jack of all trades, master of none, with all activities outside. I feel as though I could be a much better climber if I trained and practiced more but then I would have to let another activity go. Sometimes I think maybe I have too many outdoor interests, but I am never willing to give one up to improve my skills at another.

So what types of skills are you hoping to develop in climbing??

The last few years I have been leading sport climbs, which has been really fun. I would love to continue to practice this but ultimately, I would like to get into trad climbing more. It seems like this style of climbing has the potential to open up a lot more big walls in the form of multi-pitch.

Tell us a little bit about your job - what's a Health Coach?

I work as the manager and health coach at Profile by Sanford. It is a weight loss program designed by physicians and researchers at Sanford Health Systems.

Interesting! How are you staying active these days?

These days I have been doing a lot of trail running.

After the stay-at-home is lift, are you looking for any partners to climb with?

It is always fun climbing with new people!
