Meet our August WCW Corinne! Corinne is a weekend-warrior, school social worker, and a new mom! Trust us - she crushes it at ALL of these. Read on to learn how she's navigating parenthood during a pandemic and introducing Baby to all of the adventures that Corinne enjoys!

Fast Facts:

Job: School Social Worker at Harbor City International School
Originally from: Born and raised in St Paul
Favorite hobbies: Biking, rock climbing, hiking, yoga, canoeing, sailing, camping, skiing, knitting, anything that involves my doggies... oh and the baby
Favorite route: Rapprochement at Palisade Head
Why she's a Vertigal: I love meeting other women who enjoy and share interests. I would love to start a Vertimoms group one day soon
First thing she'd buy with 1 million dollars? A really nice camping trailer with AC!

How did you start climbing?

My dad introduced me to climbing when I was in middle school. I started climbing at Vertical Endeavors St Paul. From there I went to a number of camps that including climbing trips. My first experience climbing outside was at Shovel Point when I was in 7th grade. I love climbing up the North Shore! The climbing up there is one of the main reasons we moved to Duluth.

2020 has been a wild year, for lots of reasons, but you had a baby in 2020! Congrats! Tell us about having a baby in a pandemic?!
It is a very weird time to bring a human into the world! While we were at the hospital we pretty much forgot about the pandemic. We were not planning on having visitors at the hospital, so none of our plans changed in regards to the hospital stay. I was very worried that Kelly was not going to be able to be a part of the birth, but thankfully I was allowed one support person. But once we left the hospital it was very hard. When we pictured bringing our baby home from the hospital we pictured having family and close friends surrounding us to offer support and love our baby. When we got home we felt very isolated and I became very sad. I was mostly sad that others couldn't hold or meet Riley. After a few weeks and we learned more about how the virus spread we started having family meet him. Once all his close family members had been introduced and were able to hold Riley I started feeling so much better about the situation. We were so thankful that our families were being safe which made us feel good about the introduction. Over the weeks we have been introducing him to close friends we know are being safe. We just love watching him meet all of the wonderful people in our lives.

How has becoming a mom changed or impacted any of your climbing goals?
Climbing has become a family activity for us. A new climbing goal of mine is balancing climbing and new baby. My goal is to continue being passionate about climbing while introducing climbing to baby. It has been an adjustment to slow down a bit, but it has been so fun to watch him become more comfortable with climbing life.
A big focus since April is our new baby. A major focus has been introducing him to new and exciting adventures and watching him grow to love being outside. It is so fun to introduce him to adventures we love and watch him learn more and more! I cannot wait to see what he enjoys doing and what interests we will share.

How did you introduce the babe to climbing?

He’s been sitting out by our backyard bouldering wall with Kelly. We have him touch the holds on the bouldering wall with his hands and feet. He’s been up the shore to watch some climbing, touch the rocks and ropes with his hands and feet. Kelly is also doing babies and boulders with some of his friends and they are taking the babies out to bouldering spots around Duluth. The babies sit in their bouncer seats and the dads climb... I haven’t mustered the courage to do that yet!

Is there any activity that will be off limits to Riley as he gets older??

I can’t think of any activities that would be off limits to him, but there are some activities I would be disappointed if he participated in (ie football, hockey). I think it is important that if he is going to participate in activities that are risky, like climbing, that he has a good safety foundation. I would want him to know his limits and how to get out of scary situations.

....Now that I think more about risky activities… I guess free climbing would most likely be off limits… and… BASE jumping…

Considering the on-going pandemic, how are you spending your free time?
I have been spending a lot of time outside doing most of our favorite adventures we did before the pandemic. Because the climbing gym was closed, my husband built a bouldering wall in the back yard. No fails or falls yet on the bouldering wall... Just Kelly ended up accidentally making it huge! Much bigger than he had planned.

Speaking of your husband, do you happen to have a new favorite local business (wink, wink)?!
Cragdog! Cragdog is a company my husband and his friend started. This business is a custom canine equipment manufacturer based in Duluth, Minnesota. They recycle climbing gear to make collars, leashes, and toys for the dog community while giving back to the climbing community.
