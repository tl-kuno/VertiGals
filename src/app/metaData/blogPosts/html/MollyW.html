Say goodbye to 2020 and HEY HEY to Molly Watkins! Molly is an Alaskan turned Duluthian who has 100 hobbies. She's a nordic skier, climber, hiker, trail runner, canoe-r, and flutist! This year she "started dabbling" in mountain biking and triathlon training! We met her at a VertiGals climb night and we love that she's always down for adventure and a challenge.
Read on to learn about our rad December WCW, Molly!

Fast Facts
Favorite MN climbing spot: Banning State Park has some fun boulder problems (and pretty views!)
Favorite crag elsewhere: Smith Rock in Terrebone, Oregon.
First time climbing: 6th grade, Kenai Middle School gymnasium.
If you had a million dollars, the first big purchase you would make is: Index fund in my Roth IRA.

Let's just jump right in (ha - photo reference) What are some climbing skills you are working on? How?
My crimp strength is pretty low. In this current COVID-y time, I am primarily working on it when carrying groceries in from the car; why make two trips when you can carry it all in one and improve your grip strength!

LOL Yes! Speaking of COVID - did you pick up a "Pandemic Project"?
I started exploring mountain biking this summer. I kept hearing it was the thing to do in Duluth, so figured it was time to give it a try! I’m still fairly cautious (I like my collar bones intact), but slowly getting the hang of it. Zooming along trails on a mountain bike is much preferable to indoors Zooming.

So many hobbies! Now adding mountain biking! Love it...why do you climb?
Climbing is an efficient strength workout, with the added benefits of improving flexibility and challenging the mind. It’s also fun to be able to set goals on routes and see progress over time.

Do you have a favorite style in climbing?
I tend to like routes with solid holds that require brute force over technical expertise. I love corners, chimneys, and routes that require running leaps to grab the first hold (always satisfying when/if finally succeed!)

Dyno's for DAYS?! We love that!! Do you have any climbing goals you are working towards?
I would love to free climb Palisade Head someday. The outdoor climbing I have done has mostly occurred unintentionally on hikes when I should have read the map more closely. But Palisade Head looks pretty spectacular. And I figure there is no better way to gain outdoor climbing skills than having the only way out be up!

Palisade Head is our JAM here at North Shore VertiGals...but it can definitely be a little intimidating or scary for new outdoor climbers. Have you ever had a super spooky experience here in MN?

This summer I ran into a bear on the Superior Hiking Trail while running alone in the dark with dim headlight batteries (last minute decision to make my virtual Grandma’s Marathon attempt before the deadline). I’ve heard Duluth bears are much friendlier than Alaska bears, but it definitely was startling when I rounded the corner and saw glowing eyeballs 20 feet away! Fortunately, after a brief staring contest it headed off into the woods. Made for a rather jumpy next few miles, though.

Oh yes - you are from Alaska! So cool...you must feel right at home here in winter, specifically. Do you have a favorite winter activity?
Nordic skiing (especially classic skiing, and especially classic skiing on gently rolling hills).

Gentle hills on skis is bae, if you ask me. Where's the best place to ski around here, if we ask you?
The Birkie trail in Wisconsin is hard to beat as far as well-groomed trails that go on forever. Closer to home I enjoy skiing at Lester Park. It has lit trails (I’m a night owl) that can look quite magical during a snowfall. 

You are Birkie FIEND! How did it go last year??
It went pretty well! A fast course and nice sunny day. I didn’t have many long skis under my belt last year, so was worried it would be a rough second half. But in the end I still had a bit left in the tank; must have been the bonus strength from climbing! I’ve done 9 so far. Definitely plan to do more. Working towards 20 to become a Birchlegger!

[For some background, the Birkie is the American Birkebeiner. It's the largest cross-country ski race in North America, and one of the longest. #NotAnAd but the Birkie is pretty sweet, just sayin.]

When you aren't climbing, skiing, biking (or apparently unicycling) you are a Physical Therapist. Let's end this by having you shout out for us your favorite stretch or exercise**!

For climbers I often recommend strengthening the wrist extensors. Climbing requires a lot of wrist and finger flexion strength, but grip strength can be improved (and injury risk reduced) if wrist extensors are strong as well. Reverse wrist curls with a dumbbell and finger extension against a thick rubber band are good ones to start with. For runners and skiers, hip abductors are often the weakest link (keep up the bridges, single leg step-downs, and clamshell exercises!)
