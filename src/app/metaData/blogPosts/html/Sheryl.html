Interview written and compiled by Stephanie Hall
This weeks WCW is a badass not just because of the grades she’s climbing but because she still finds time to climb in the midst of the full life she leads outside of climbing. Sheryl Filby Williams (@sherylfilby) is one of those people that you always wonder how she possibly has enough time to do all of the things she does. She is a Senior Hydrogeologist and Vice President at Barr Engineering Co. here in Duluth, she’s a wife and a mom to two active kiddos, a mountain biker, hiker, she crews on a sailboat for Wednesday night races, takes her kids on adventures near and far, and somehow she still finds time to climb. Her energy and enthusiasm is infectious, and 20 years of climbing experience and her life experiences make her a great partner for a mid-day climb session. What made you join VertiGals? I was really excited to see a women’s climbing group start in Duluth.  Through my career as a woman scientist, I’ve seen how important it can be for women to support each other in areas they have historically been underrepresented. My daughter looks up to the great women climbers in Duluth so much; she is very proud to climb with the VertiGals (see Reija's Mini WCW interview below!)       [gallery ids="417,415" type="rectangular"] You've been climbing for a long time, how has climbing changed for you as you've grown older and grown your family? I first took at climbing class in college (over 20 years ago) and climbing has been a part of my life since then. Climbing has always been a great way to socialize and stay strong.  My climbing involvement has ebbed and flowed over the years but I always seem to come back to it. Currently, I’m fortunate to work for a great company where I have flexible work time, so I can sneak in a workout during the day at VE when time allows.  I also try to bring my kids in the evenings. It’s hard to fit everything in with a career, personal interests, and family, but by sharing my passions with my kids, I can combine time on the wall with family time. [caption id="attachment_416" align="alignnone" width="640"] Ice Climbing in Bozeman, MT in 2002[/caption] My kids have been climbing since they were really young- it’s one of my greatest joys as a parent to share my passion for outdoors activities with my kids. When I’m with my kids, the climbing is all about them – keeping it light and fun.  But I still love to climb just for me. I will always enjoy pushing myself mentally and physically. As a mother, how do you think your daughter's experience in the outdoors will be different than your own? What do you hope is different? I grew up being considered a “tomboy.”  I was very outdoorsy and athletic. I think the difference now is that my outdoorsy and athletic girl (age 7) can be just that – an outdoorsy and athletic girl – who doesn’t have to associate any part of that being for boys (and she does still love pink and dressing up).  Both my kids have gravitated towards the outdoors. Nothing makes me happier as a parent to be out with my family on the trail backpacking or skiing together. We have been very intentional about bringing our kids on outdoor adventures from a young age – now it’s just part of their lives.

What do you like about climbing?  "It’s fun! I mean, it’s very active and I like to get stronger." What do you want to climb when you get older? "More of the same. Inside is kind of my jam." What has been your favorite climbing experience?  "Cleaning a 5.8!"
What changes good or bad have you seen in the Duluth outdoor community? Duluth has always had a strong outdoor culture, but it’s only grown more vibrant.  I feel very fortunate to live in a place where my kids’ sports are skiing, mountain biking, and climbing.  We live right on Lester Park and the ability to be on the trails, river, or big lake right from our house is so unique and incredible. What are some of your other non-climbing hobbies/passions? In the summer we like to mountain bike and enjoy local swimming holes.  I also crew on a sailboat for Wednesday night races. In the winter, we do all kinds of skiing. I picked up telemark skiing last winter so that’s my latest challenge.  My family loves to travel and we just finished our first family backpacking trip in Zion National Park in October. My husband and I also backpacked in the Dolomites in Italy a couple years ago. Be on the lookout for a family VertiGals event coming up soon! We want to make sure we are inclusive to moms and youngsters, all are welcome to attend. Remember you are welcome to join even if you can't come every month or if you want bring your kids, we are open to all!
