Many of you probably recognize this weeks WCW Claire @clairepoppendeck; she used to be one of the friendly faces that welcomed you to VE-Duluth, or maybe you've seen her climbing hard, training, or dry-tooling in the gym, but did you know she is also a full-time student? She has only been climbing for about three years but she grew up going to circus camp learning aerials and contortion (she even had a rope and aerial hoop in her backyard), and the need to climb and play eventually lead her to the UMD climbing wall once she got to college. Currently she's working on her degree in Special Education and Elementary Education, but she's still finding time to train and climbing competitively. In November Claire took 2nd place at the UMD Drytool comp in the advanced category, and most recently Claire qualified for the 2019 USA World Cup Ice Climbing Team, and will compete in the Denver Ice Climbing World cup event in both Lead and and Speed. Claire talks about getting into competitive climbing, ice climbing, and shares some advice in the interview below. Click here if you want to help support the USA Ice Climbing team. What do you love about climbing?

I love that climbing is always a physical and mental challenge. Even when you accomplish something, there is always something harder to do. I also love the local climbing community. Everyone is so kind and willing to share their knowledge and time with one another.

How did you get into ice climbing/dry tooling?

I started ice climbing last year at the UMD drytool comp. I was a little unsure about it at first, but then I went outside a few times and I was hooked.

How did you get into competition climbing? 

Two years ago started participating in a few rock climbing comps. Last year I had a lot more time, so I went to a few comps in the Wisconsin Indoor Climbing Series, the UMD drytool comp, and several other events. In March I went to the 2018 North American Ice Climbing Championships. At this competition I had my first experience with speed climbing and kicking into plywood. The best part was meeting athletes who regularly compete in ice climbing. They inspired me to apply for the US ice climbing team.

How do you train differently for rock climbing vs ice climbing?

This summer when I was training for rock climbing, I climbed outside as much as I could. I also tried to climb in the gym frequently. I was not training for anything specific, so I was just trying to have fun and improve my skills. For ice climbing, I have been doing kettle bell training 2-3 days a week (similar to cross-fit). My drytooling/ice climbing schedule has been more random, but I get out at least a couple times a week. I try to do a few swimming and running workouts every now and then as well.

How are you hoping to grow as a climber or what skills are you hoping to develop? My current ice climbing goals are to lead ice, climb multi-pitch ice routes, and become more competent in leading drytool routes. For rock climbing, I want to become a more diverse climber, and I want to do some multi-pitches. But at the end of the day, my greatest goal is just to have fun, meet new people, and gain confidence in myself. In this whole training process, I am learning that it does not matter how I climb at any competition, or how I compare to anyone else. Each and every small accomplishment that I experience along the way matters. It is about personal growth and trusting The Lord through every clip and every micro-pullup!
